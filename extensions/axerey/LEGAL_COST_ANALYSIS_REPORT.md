## I. Case Context and Background

### A. The Underlying Dispute

**Election Contest Proceedings:**
- Petitioner Jeff Buongiorno was a candidate in the 2024 election for Palm Beach County Supervisor of Elections
- Following the election, Petitioner initiated an election contest action in the Circuit Court of Palm Beach County (Case No. 50-2024-CA-011351)
- The circuit court entered final judgment on July 8, 2025, against Petitioner
- Petitioner timely filed a notice of appeal to the Fourth District Court of Appeal (Case No. 4D2025-2293)

### B. The Record Preparation Crisis

**The Clerk's Failures:**
1. **Initial Record Transmission (September 25, 2025):** Clerk transmitted Record on Appeal with Certificate of Compliance certifying completeness, but omitted approximately **32 docketed Document Instrument Numbers (DINs)**

2. **Subsequent Transmissions (October 6, 2025; December 1, 2025):** Clerk issued additional Certificates of Compliance, each time certifying the record as complete despite continued omissions

3. **Volume and Scope:**
   - Original Record on Appeal: **Over 5,200 pages**
   - Supplemental Record: **Approximately 138 pages**
   - Omitted filings span from November 29, 2024, to September 25, 2025
   - Includes motions, responses, affidavits, hearing notices, court orders, receipts, and transcripts

4. **Unauthorized Alterations:** Record contained redactions not reflected on progress docket and not authorized by court order, violating Florida Rule of General Practice and Judicial Administration 2.420

### C. Exhaustion of Appellate Remedies

**District Court Proceedings:**
- Petitioner filed multiple motions pursuant to Florida Rule of Appellate Procedure 9.200(f) seeking supplementation and correction
- Fourth District Court of Appeal granted only limited relief, ordering inclusion of **3 of the 32 omitted filings**
- Court declined to order reconciliation of record to progress docket or withdrawal of false certificates
- After partial supplementation, Clerk issued yet another false Certificate of Compliance on December 1, 2025
- **No further mechanism exists within ordinary appellate process** to compel compliance

## II. Legal Complexity Analysis

### A. Constitutional Jurisdiction Issues

**Original Jurisdiction Analysis:**
- **Florida Constitution Article V, Section 3(b)(8):** Grants Florida Supreme Court original jurisdiction to issue writs of mandamus
- **Jurisdictional Challenge:** Must establish that this is not a disguised attempt to review district court decisions
- **Legal Research Required:**
  - Distinction between supervisory mandamus and case-level Rule 9.200(f) relief
  - When ministerial duties of constitutional clerk justify original jurisdiction
  - Precedent on mandamus to compel record preparation
  - **Estimated Research Time: 8-12 hours**

### B. Mandamus Legal Framework

**Huffman v. State Three-Prong Test:**
1. **Clear Legal Right:** Petitioner's right to complete record under Fla. R. App. P. 9.200(a)(1)
2. **Clear Ministerial Duty:** Clerk's nondiscretionary obligations under Rule 9.200(d)
3. **No Adequate Remedy at Law:** Exhaustion of Rule 9.200(f) remedies

**Supporting Case Law Research:**
- *Huffman v. State*, 813 So. 2d 10 (Fla. 2000) - mandamus standard
- *Pace v. Singletary*, 633 So. 2d 516 (Fla. 1st DCA 1994) - clerk's ministerial duties
- Related mandamus jurisprudence on record preparation
- **Estimated Research Time: 4-6 hours**

### C. Procedural Rules Analysis

**Multiple Overlapping Rules:**
1. **Florida Rule of Appellate Procedure 9.200:**
   - Subsection (a)(1): Right to complete record
   - Subsection (d): Clerk's ministerial duties
   - Subsection (f): Supplementation procedures and limitations

2. **Florida Rule of General Practice and Judicial Administration 2.420:**
   - Subsection (d)(1)-(2): Requirements for confidentiality/redaction
   - Establishes clerk cannot unilaterally alter or omit records

3. **Florida Statutes:**
   - Section 28.13: Clerk's duty to maintain all papers
   - Section 28.24(3): Fees for record preparation (ministerial nature)

**Rule Interpretation and Application:**
- When Rule 9.200(f) relief is exhausted
- Distinction between ministerial and discretionary duties
- Certification requirements and withdrawal procedures
- **Estimated Research Time: 3-5 hours**

### D. Record Reconciliation Complexity

**Document Analysis Required:**
- Compare 5,200+ page Record on Appeal against complete progress docket
- Identify 32 omitted Document Instrument Numbers
- Verify filing dates, descriptions, and status (stricken/sealed)
- Document unauthorized redactions
- Create detailed exhibit with 32 tabs (Exhibit D)
- **Estimated Time: 12-18 hours**

### E. Procedural History Documentation

**Chronological Documentation:**
- Multiple record transmissions (Sept 25, Oct 6, Dec 1, 2025)
- District court orders and limited relief granted
- Timeline of motions, responses, and certifications
- Exhaustion of remedies analysis
- **Estimated Time: 4-6 hours**
