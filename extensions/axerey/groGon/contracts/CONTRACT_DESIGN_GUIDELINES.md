# Contract Design Guidelines

**Best Practices for GroGon Contracts**

---

## Design Principles

### 1. Clarity and Readability

**Typography:**
- Use clear, professional fonts (Arial, Calibri, or Times New Roman)
- Minimum 11pt font size for body text
- 14-16pt for headings
- Adequate line spacing (1.15-1.5)

**Structure:**
- Numbered sections and subsections
- Clear headings and subheadings
- Bullet points for lists
- Tables for pricing/comparisons
- White space for visual breathing room

**Language:**
- Plain English where possible
- Define technical terms
- Avoid unnecessary legal jargon
- Use active voice

---

### 2. Visual Hierarchy

**Document Structure:**
```
1. Header (Company name, logo, document type)
2. Parties section (clearly identified)
3. Main content (numbered sections)
4. Signature block (prominent, clear)
5. Footer (contract number, dates, status)
```

**Emphasis:**
- Bold for section headings
- Italics for defined terms
- Underline for critical dates/amounts
- Boxes or borders for signature areas

---

### 3. Professional Formatting

**Page Layout:**
- 1-inch margins minimum
- Page numbers (bottom center)
- Header with company name/logo
- Consistent spacing throughout

**Color Usage:**
- Black text on white background (for printing)
- Company colors for headers/footers (optional)
- Red or bold for critical warnings
- Professional, not flashy

**Tables:**
- Use tables for pricing, specifications, checklists
- Clear column headers
- Alternating row colors for readability
- Borders for clarity

---

### 4. Essential Elements

**Every Contract Should Include:**

1. **Clear Identification**
   - Contract number
   - Date created
   - Status (Draft/Active/Completed)
   - Version number (if applicable)

2. **Party Information**
   - Full legal names
   - Complete addresses
   - Contact information
   - Authorized signatories

3. **Key Terms Summary Box**
   - Service/product description
   - Total amount
   - Key dates
   - Payment terms
   - (Place at top for quick reference)

4. **Signature Block**
   - Clear signature lines
   - Printed name lines
   - Title lines
   - Date lines
   - Space for both parties

5. **Legal Protections**
   - Governing law
   - Jurisdiction
   - Dispute resolution
   - Limitation of liability
   - Termination clauses

---

### 5. User Experience

**Checkboxes:**
- Use checkboxes for options/selections
- Makes contracts interactive and clear
- Easy to see what's included/excluded

**Fill-in Fields:**
- Use brackets [ ] or underscores _____ for fill-in fields
- Clear labels for what goes in each field
- Consistent formatting

**Sections:**
- Logical flow of information
- Related items grouped together
- Easy to navigate with table of contents for long contracts

---

### 6. Digital Considerations

**PDF Format:**
- All contracts should be PDFs for final versions
- Editable Word/Google Docs for templates
- Fillable PDF forms for online completion

**Digital Signatures:**
- Support for DocuSign, HelloSign, or similar
- Clear instructions for e-signature process
- Legal validity confirmed

**Version Control:**
- Track all versions
- Date and version number on each document
- Archive old versions

---

### 7. Industry-Specific Elements

**For Drone Services:**
- Weather contingency clauses
- Airspace clearance requirements
- Safety protocols
- Insurance requirements
- Regulatory compliance statements

**For Product Sales:**
- Warranty terms clearly stated
- Return policy
- Training/support included
- Compliance certifications listed
- Serial number tracking

---

## Template Structure

### Standard Contract Sections:

1. **Header** - Company branding, document type
2. **Parties** - Who is involved
3. **Recitals** - Background/context (optional)
4. **Terms** - Main agreement terms
5. **Payment** - Financial terms
6. **Delivery/Performance** - When/how
7. **Warranties** - What's guaranteed
8. **Limitations** - What's not covered
9. **Termination** - How to end agreement
10. **General Provisions** - Legal boilerplate
11. **Signatures** - Execution block

---

## Quality Checklist

Before finalizing any contract:

- [ ] All parties clearly identified
- [ ] All amounts and dates filled in
- [ ] Payment terms clearly stated
- [ ] Delivery/service dates specified
- [ ] Warranties and limitations clear
- [ ] Signature blocks complete
- [ ] Contract number assigned
- [ ] Version control information included
- [ ] Legal review completed (if required)
- [ ] Both parties have copies

---

## Brand Consistency

**Use GroGon Branding:**
- Company logo in header
- Company colors (primary blue, accent colors)
- Professional, modern design
- Consistent with website/marketing materials

**Tone:**
- Professional but approachable
- Clear and direct
- Fair to both parties
- Reflects company values (security, quality, compliance)

---

## Examples of Good Contract Design

**What Works:**
- Clear section headers
- Summary box at top
- Checkboxes for options
- Tables for pricing
- White space for readability
- Consistent formatting

**What to Avoid:**
- Dense blocks of text
- Tiny font sizes
- Unclear language
- Missing information
- Inconsistent formatting
- Hard-to-find key terms
