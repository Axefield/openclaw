# Contract Contrasts & Comparisons

**Understanding Different Contract Types and Approaches for GroGon**

---

## Service Contracts vs. Product Sales Contracts

### Key Differences

| Aspect | Service Agreement | Product Sales Agreement |
|--------|------------------|------------------------|
| **Primary Focus** | Performance of work | Transfer of goods |
| **Payment Timing** | Often milestone-based or upon completion | Typically upfront or upon delivery |
| **Deliverables** | Intangible (photos, reports, data) | Tangible (drone hardware) |
| **Warranty** | Service quality guarantee | Product defect warranty |
| **Risk Transfer** | Risk during service performance | Risk upon delivery/acceptance |
| **Termination** | Can terminate mid-service | Usually final sale |
| **Intellectual Property** | Often shared or licensed | Usually transfers with product |
| **Ongoing Relationship** | May include support/maintenance | Separate support agreement |

---

## One-Time vs. Recurring Service Contracts

### One-Time Service Contract
**Characteristics:**
- Single project or service event
- Fixed scope and timeline
- Payment upon completion
- No ongoing obligations
- Example: Single property photography job

**Best For:**
- First-time customers
- Specific projects
- Testing the relationship
- One-off needs

### Recurring Service Contract
**Characteristics:**
- Ongoing service relationship
- Monthly/quarterly/annual terms
- Retainer or subscription model
- Service level agreements (SLAs)
- Automatic renewal clauses
- Example: Monthly inspection services

**Best For:**
- Established customers
- Regular maintenance needs
- Predictable revenue
- Long-term relationships

---

## Fixed-Price vs. Time-and-Materials Contracts

### Fixed-Price Contract
**Structure:**
- Agreed total price upfront
- Scope clearly defined
- Risk on service provider
- Client knows exact cost

**Advantages:**
- Predictable for client
- Incentivizes efficiency
- Clear budget

**Disadvantages:**
- Risk of scope creep
- Less flexibility
- May need change orders

**Example:**
"$1,500 for complete property photography package including 50 photos and 2-minute video"

### Time-and-Materials Contract
**Structure:**
- Hourly/daily rate
- Materials/expenses billed separately
- Actual time tracked
- Final cost may vary

**Advantages:**
- Flexible scope
- Fair for uncertain projects
- Easy to adjust

**Disadvantages:**
- Unpredictable cost
- Requires trust
- More administration

**Example:**
"$200/hour for inspection services, plus $50/hour for equipment, plus travel expenses"

## Standard vs. Custom Contracts

### Standard Contract Template
**Characteristics:**
- Pre-written terms
- Fill-in-the-blank format
- Quick to execute
- Consistent terms
- Lower legal review cost

**Use When:**
- Standard service offerings
- Similar projects
- Quick turnaround needed
- Lower-value transactions

### Custom Contract
**Characteristics:**
- Negotiated terms
- Specific to project
- Attorney-drafted
- Unique provisions
- Higher legal cost

**Use When:**
- Large projects ($50K+)
- Complex requirements
- Government contracts
- Strategic partnerships
- Unique risk factors

---

## B2B vs. B2C Contract Approaches

### Business-to-Business (B2B)
**Characteristics:**
- More detailed terms
- Negotiation expected
- Longer payment terms
- Volume discounts
- Custom pricing
- Formal legal language
- Purchase orders accepted

**Tone:**
- Professional
- Detailed
- Comprehensive
- Legally precise

### Business-to-Consumer (B2C)
**Characteristics:**
- Simplified language
- Standard terms
- Payment upfront or credit card
- Consumer protection focus
- Clear cancellation rights
- Plain English explanations

**Tone:**
- Friendly but professional
- Easy to understand
- Consumer-friendly
- Transparent

---

## Government vs. Commercial Contracts

### Government Contracts
**Characteristics:**
- NDAA compliance required
- Federal Acquisition Regulation (FAR) compliance
- Detailed specifications
- Strict compliance requirements
- Longer procurement process
- Payment terms per government schedule
- Audit rights
- Termination for convenience clauses

**Key Requirements:**
- U.S. manufacturing
- Security clearances (if applicable)
- Detailed reporting
- Compliance certifications
- Insurance requirements

### Commercial Contracts
**Characteristics:**
- More flexible terms
- Faster negotiation
- Standard commercial terms
- Direct negotiation
- Customizable
- Faster payment

**Key Focus:**
- Service quality
- Price competitiveness
- Relationship building
- Flexibility

---

## Domestic vs. International Contracts

### Domestic (U.S.) Contracts
**Governing Law:** U.S. state law (typically Florida)
**Currency:** USD
**Language:** English
**Dispute Resolution:** U.S. courts or arbitration
**Compliance:** U.S. regulations (FAA, FCC, NDAA)

### International Contracts
**Governing Law:** Choice of law clause needed
**Currency:** May need foreign currency
**Language:** May need translation
**Dispute Resolution:** International arbitration
**Compliance:** Multiple jurisdictions
**Export Controls:** ITAR/EAR compliance
**Tax:** VAT, customs duties

**Note:** GroGon's NDAA-compliant products may have export restrictions

## When to Use Each Approach

### Use Standard Templates When:
- ✅ Transaction value < $10,000
- ✅ Standard service/product offering
- ✅ Similar to previous contracts
- ✅ Quick turnaround needed
- ✅ Low risk profile
- ✅ Established customer

### Use Custom Contracts When:
- ✅ Transaction value > $50,000
- ✅ Unique requirements
- ✅ High risk profile
- ✅ Government contracts
- ✅ Strategic partnerships
- ✅ Complex deliverables
- ✅ Long-term agreements

### Use Hybrid Approach When:
- ✅ Transaction value $10,000 - $50,000
- ✅ Standard template with custom addendums
- ✅ Negotiate specific terms only
- ✅ Moderate risk

---

## Best Practices for Contract Selection

1. **Start Simple:** Use standard templates for initial relationships
2. **Scale Up:** Move to custom contracts as relationship/value grows
3. **Know Your Risk:** Higher risk = more detailed contract
4. **Consider Relationship:** Long-term = more flexible terms
5. **Legal Review:** Always review contracts > $25K or with unique terms
6. **Document Decisions:** Note why you chose each approach
7. **Learn and Improve:** Update templates based on experience

---

## Contract Evolution Path

**Typical Customer Journey:**

1. **First Contact:** Standard service agreement (one-time)
2. **Repeat Customer:** Standard service agreement (recurring option)
3. **Growing Relationship:** Custom terms for volume
4. **Product Purchase:** Product sales agreement
5. **Partnership:** Custom partnership agreement
6. **Strategic Account:** Master service agreement with work orders

**Contract Sophistication Increases with:**
- Relationship length
- Transaction value
- Risk level
- Strategic importance
- Customer requirements
