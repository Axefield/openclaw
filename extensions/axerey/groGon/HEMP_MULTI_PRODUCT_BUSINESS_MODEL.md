# Hemp Multi-Product Manufacturing Business Model
## GroGon - Fast-to-Market Hemp Processing & Drone Manufacturing

**Date**: December 2025  
**Location**: Davie, Florida

---

## Executive Summary

GroGon operates a vertically integrated hemp processing facility that produces multiple revenue-generating products while developing drone-specific materials:

1. **Hemp Seed Production & Distribution** - Grow and distribute seeds to supplier farmers
2. **Hemp Paper** - Fast-to-market product with established demand
3. **Hemp Ethanol** - Biofuel for local and national markets
4. **Hemp-Based Bioplastics** - Sustainable plastic alternatives
5. **Hemp Composite Materials** - For drone manufacturing
6. **Commercial Drones** - Using our own hemp-based materials
7. **Drone Services** - Using our sustainable equipment

**Strategy**: Start with seed production and paper to generate revenue quickly (3-6 months), then add ethanol and bioplastics, while using those same processing capabilities and materials for drone manufacturing. Seed production ensures quality control and creates a revenue stream from supplier relationships.

## 1. Hemp Processing Products & Equipment

### 1.1 Hemp Paper Production

**Market Opportunity:**
- Growing demand for sustainable paper products
- Hemp paper is stronger and more durable than wood paper
- Premium pricing for eco-friendly products
- Can start selling within 3-6 months

**Production Process:**
1. **Harvesting**: Collect hemp stalks
2. **Decortication**: Separate bast fibers (outer) and hurd fibers (inner)
3. **Pulping**: Process hurd fibers into pulp slurry
4. **Pressing & Drying**: Form paper sheets
5. **Rolling & Cutting**: Create finished paper products

**Equipment Needed:**
- **Hemp Decorticator**: $50,000 - $150,000
  - Separates bast and hurd fibers
  - Can process 2-5 tons per hour
- **Pulping Equipment**: $75,000 - $200,000
  - Processes fibers into pulp
  - Includes mixing tanks and slurry preparation
- **Paper Press & Dryer**: $100,000 - $250,000
  - Forms and dries paper sheets
  - Industrial rollers for large-scale production
- **Cutting Equipment**: $25,000 - $50,000
  - Final cutting and packaging

**Total Paper Equipment**: $250,000 - $650,000

**Production Capacity:**
- Small scale: 5-10 tons paper/month
- Medium scale: 20-50 tons paper/month
- Large scale: 100+ tons paper/month

**Revenue Potential:**
- Hemp paper: $2,000 - $4,000 per ton
- Year 1: 60-120 tons = $120,000 - $480,000
- Year 2: 240-600 tons = $480,000 - $2,400,000

### 1.2 Hemp Ethanol Production

**Market Opportunity:**
- Biofuel market growing rapidly
- Government incentives for renewable fuels
- Can sell to fuel distributors, local gas stations
- Higher value product than paper

**Production Process:**
1. **Pretreatment**: Grind hemp biomass, break down cellulose
2. **Enzymatic Hydrolysis**: Convert cellulose to fermentable sugars
3. **Fermentation**: Yeast converts sugars to ethanol
4. **Distillation**: Purify ethanol (95-99% pure)
5. **Dehydration**: Remove remaining water (if needed)

**Equipment Needed:**
- **Grinders/Mills**: $30,000 - $75,000
  - Prepare biomass for processing
- **Pretreatment System**: $100,000 - $200,000
  - Breaks down cellulose structure
- **Fermentation Tanks**: $50,000 - $150,000
  - Multiple tanks for continuous production
  - Temperature and pH control
- **Distillation Unit**: $150,000 - $400,000
  - Purifies ethanol
  - Can process 50-40,000 gallons/day (depending on size)
- **Storage Tanks**: $25,000 - $75,000
  - Store finished ethanol

**Total Ethanol Equipment**: $355,000 - $900,000

**Production Capacity:**
- Small scale: 1,000-5,000 gallons/month
- Medium scale: 10,000-25,000 gallons/month
- Large scale: 50,000+ gallons/month

**Revenue Potential:**
- Hemp ethanol: $2.50 - $3.50 per gallon
- Year 1: 12,000-60,000 gallons = $30,000 - $210,000
- Year 2: 120,000-300,000 gallons = $300,000 - $1,050,000

**Recommendation**: Start with Option B or C for guaranteed supply

### 5.3 Supplier Payment Terms & Structure

**Payment Methods:**
- **ACH Transfer**: Preferred method (fast, low cost)
- **Check**: Traditional, acceptable for smaller suppliers
- **Wire Transfer**: For large payments or international suppliers
- **Cryptocurrency**: Optional for tech-forward suppliers (future consideration)

**Payment Terms Options:**

**Option 1: Net 30 (Standard)**
- Payment due 30 days after delivery
- Most common for established relationships
- Allows time for quality inspection
- **Best for**: Reliable suppliers, regular deliveries

**Option 2: Net 15 (Fast Pay)**
- Payment due 15 days after delivery
- Incentive for preferred suppliers
- Faster cash flow for farmers
- **Best for**: High-quality suppliers, volume commitments

**Option 3: Net 60 (Extended Terms)**
- Payment due 60 days after delivery
- Used for large volume contracts
- Helps manage cash flow
- **Best for**: Major suppliers, seasonal contracts

**Option 4: Advance Payment (Pre-Pay)**
- Partial or full payment before delivery
- 25-50% deposit upon contract signing
- Remaining balance upon delivery
- **Best for**: New suppliers, securing large contracts, seasonal planning

**Option 5: Milestone Payments**
- Payment at planting: 20-30%
- Payment at harvest: 40-50%
- Final payment upon delivery: 20-30%
- **Best for**: Large acreage contracts, multi-season agreements

**Recommended Standard Terms:**
- **New Suppliers**: Net 30 with 25% advance payment
- **Established Suppliers**: Net 15-30 based on relationship
- **Large Contracts**: Milestone payments or Net 60
- **Seasonal Contracts**: Advance payment to secure supply

### 5.4 Pricing Structure for Supplier Contracts

**Base Pricing Model:**

**Tier 1: Premium Quality** ($400 - $450 per ton)
- High fiber content (>20% fiber)
- Low moisture (<12%)
- Clean, minimal debris
- Certified organic (if applicable)
- Consistent quality across deliveries

**Tier 2: Standard Quality** ($300 - $350 per ton)
- Standard fiber content (15-20%)
- Acceptable moisture (12-15%)
- Minor debris acceptable
- Good quality, consistent

**Tier 3: Basic Quality** ($250 - $300 per ton)
- Lower fiber content (10-15%)
- Higher moisture (15-18%)
- More debris, requires more processing
- Acceptable for ethanol production

**Quality-Based Adjustments:**
- **Fiber Content Bonus**: +$10-20/ton for >20% fiber
- **Moisture Penalty**: -$5-10/ton for >15% moisture
- **Organic Premium**: +$50-100/ton for certified organic
- **Volume Discount**: -$10-20/ton for 100+ ton contracts
- **Early Delivery Bonus**: +$5-10/ton for delivery before harvest season

**Contract Pricing Examples:**

**Example 1: Small Supplier (10-50 tons)**
- Base rate: $300-350/ton
- Payment: Net 30
- Quality: Standard
- **Total Cost**: $3,000 - $17,500 per supplier

**Example 2: Medium Supplier (50-200 tons)**
- Base rate: $325-375/ton (volume discount)
- Payment: Net 15-30
- Quality: Premium
- **Total Cost**: $16,250 - $75,000 per supplier

**Example 3: Large Supplier (200+ tons)**
- Base rate: $350-400/ton (volume discount)
- Payment: Milestone or Net 60
- Quality: Premium
- **Total Cost**: $70,000+ per supplier
