Steelman Analysis: Strengthening Arguments

Steelman analysis strengthens arguments by finding their most charitable, strongest version:

How It Works

  • Charitable Assumptions: Adds reasonable assumptions to strengthen the argument
  • Premise Support: Identifies and strengthens weak premises
  • Objection Addressing: Proactively addresses likely objections
  • Evidence Integration: Incorporates concrete evidence to support claims

Strawman Analysis: Identifying Weak Points

Strawman analysis identifies distortions, fallacies, and weak points in arguments:

How It Works

  • Distortion Detection: Identifies exaggerations, oversimplifications, misattributions
  • Fallacy Identification: Finds logical fallacies (strawman, ad hominem, false dichotomy, etc.)
  • Weak Premise Detection: Identifies premises that need more support
  • Improvement Hints: Provides guidance on how to strengthen the argument

Common Issues Identified

  • Appeal to Common Sense: Claims that assume obvious truth without evidence
  • Appeal to Popularity: Claims that assume everyone knows something
  • False Dichotomy: Binary thinking that oversimplifies complex issues

Investment Application

Used to identify weak points in investment arguments, ensuring claims are well-supported and nuanced, not relying on appeals to common sense or popularity.

Mind-Balance: Probabilistic Decision-Making

Mind-balance provides probabilistic decision-making with abstention-aware scoring using an angel/demon advisory system:

How It Works

  • Angel Signal: Ethical grounding, harmonizing influence from stable cosine dynamics
  • Demon Signal: Opportunity/urgency, escalating urgency and emotional pull
  • Probabilistic Mode: Statistical analysis with p(positive) and p(negative) probabilities
  • Blend Mode: Weighted vector field combining both perspectives
  • Confidence Threshold: Requires 0.700 confidence for decision (important for trading logic)

Parameters

  • theta (0.0-1.0): Optimism level (higher = more optimistic)
  • phi (0.0-1.0): Risk tolerance (higher = more risk-tolerant)
  • cosine (0.0-1.0): Similarity to past decisions (higher = more similar)
  • tangent (0.0-1.0): Complexity factor (higher = more complex)
  • mode: angel, demon, blend, or probabilistic
