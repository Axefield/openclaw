## Where Your Donation Goes: The Honest Breakdown

If you donate to this campaign, here's exactly where it goes:

### 🎯 **45% - Voter Outreach & Advertising** ($810K)

- Digital ads targeting voters who care about transparency, accountability, and constitutional governance
- Targeted mailers to independent and swing voters
- Social media campaigns amplifying evidence-based content
- Streaming platform ads (YouTube, Rumble, podcasts)

**Why not TV?** Because voters under 50 don't watch cable news, and voters over 50 respond better to targeted mail. TV ads are expensive ego projects.

---

### 🧠 **25% - Research & Content Creation** ($450K)

This is what makes this campaign different.

- **Investigative journalism:** Digging into public records, FOIA requests, court filings
- **Expert analysis:** Hiring cybersecurity, legal, and policy experts to vet claims
- **Content production:** High-quality video, articles, and reports that expose corruption

**Why so much on research?** Because I'm not selling slogans—I'm selling evidence. And evidence takes resources to gather and present credibly.

---

### 🛠️ **20% - Staff & Operations** ($360K)

- Campaign manager
- Field organizers (for grassroots outreach)
- Compliance officer (FEC regulations are no joke)
- Digital strategist
- Part-time legal counsel

**Why so few staff?** Because I'm not building a bureaucracy. I'm building a movement. Lean team, high competence.

---

### 🗳️ **5% - Events & Ground Game** ($90K)

- Town halls
- Door-to-door canvassing
- Voter registration drives
- Precinct captain training

**Why so little?** Because events are about showing up, not showboating. We'll do high-impact, low-cost engagement.

---

### ⚖️ **5% - Legal & Compliance** ($90K)

- FEC filings and reporting
- Legal review of advertising
- Defense against inevitable opposition research attacks

**Why necessary?** Campaign finance law is a minefield. One mistake can tank a campaign. This is non-negotiable spending.

## Why I'm Not Taking PAC Money

Here's the deal with Political Action Committees (PACs):

**They give you money. Then they own you.**

When a PAC donates $50,000, they're not doing it out of the goodness of their hearts. They're buying access and influence. And when they call, you're expected to answer.

I didn't sue my way through the election establishment just to be beholden to a different set of insiders.

**My funding rule:** Individual contributions only, capped at the federal limit ($3,300 per person for the primary and general election).

This means I need a lot of people giving smaller amounts. And that's exactly the point—**a campaign funded by the people is accountable to the people.**
