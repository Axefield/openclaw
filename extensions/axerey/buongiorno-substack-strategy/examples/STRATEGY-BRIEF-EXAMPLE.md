# Strategy Brief: How to Actually Fix Election Security (Without Making It Partisan)

## The Problem with How We Talk About Elections

Every time someone raises concerns about election integrity, the response is predictable:

- **One side says:** "You're undermining democracy! Elections are perfectly secure!"
- **The other side says:** "You're ignoring fraud! The system is rigged!"

Both responses miss the point.

The real question isn't whether the 2020 or 2022 elections were "stolen." The question is: **Can our current system prove they weren't?**

If the answer is no—if we can't definitively demonstrate security—then everyone should be concerned, regardless of party.

Today, I'm outlining a strategy to fix election security in a way that **both sides should support**. Because transparency isn't partisan. Verification isn't radical. And accountability benefits everyone.

---

## The Non-Partisan Framework for Election Security

### Principle 1: **Trust Through Verification, Not Authority**

**Current System:**  
"Trust us—we're election officials, and we say everything is secure."

**Better System:**  
"Here's the evidence showing how we secured the election. Verify it yourself."

### Principle 2: **Assume Adversarial Audits**

**Current System:**  
Security measures designed to pass friendly reviews and compliance checklists.

**Better System:**  
Security measures designed assuming a hostile actor with resources and expertise is trying to break them.

### Principle 3: **Transparency by Default, Secrecy by Exception**

**Current System:**  
"We can't release that—it might reveal security vulnerabilities."

**Better System:**  
"We're releasing everything except [specific, justified exceptions]. Here's why those exceptions are necessary."

## The Five-Pillar Reform Plan

If I'm elected to Congress, I'll introduce legislation based on these five pillars:

---

### Pillar 1: **Mandatory Paper Trail + Digital Audit**

**The Reform:**  
Every voting system must produce a voter-verifiable paper record that can be audited independently of the electronic count.

**Why It Works:**  
- **Democrats:** You get a verifiable backup that proves electronic counts are accurate
- **Republicans:** You get transparency and the ability to audit contested results
- **Everyone:** You get redundancy—if one system fails, the other provides backup

**Implementation:**
- Federal funding for states to upgrade to paper-trail systems
- Grants prioritized for jurisdictions that adopt open-source auditing tools
- Deadline: All systems compliant by 2028 midterms

**Cost:** ~$500M over 4 years (about 1/10th the cost of one F-35 fighter jet)

---

### Pillar 2: **Open-Source Voting Software**

**The Reform:**  
Require all voting system software to be open-source and publicly auditable.

**Why It Works:**  
- **Transparency:** Anyone can inspect the code for vulnerabilities or manipulation
- **Security:** More eyes on code means more people finding and fixing bugs
- **Trust:** You don't have to "believe" the vendor—you can verify yourself

**Objection:** "Won't open-source reveal vulnerabilities to hackers?"

**Response:** Security through obscurity doesn't work. Proprietary systems already get hacked—we just don't find out until it's too late. Open-source means vulnerabilities are found by good actors before bad actors exploit them.

**Implementation:**
- Phase-in period: 2026-2030
- Federal certification process for open-source voting systems
- Bounty program for security researchers who find vulnerabilities

---

### Pillar 3: **Real-Time Public Dashboards**

**The Reform:**  
Create public-facing dashboards showing election security metrics in real-time:

- Voter turnout by precinct (updated hourly)
- Number of provisional ballots
- Equipment malfunctions and resolutions
- Audit logs (anonymized) showing chain of custody

**Why It Works:**  
- **Transparency in action:** Not just releasing data after the fact—showing it live
- **Early problem detection:** Anomalies can be spotted and addressed immediately
- **Builds trust:** When you can see the process, you don't have to "trust" officials blindly

**Implementation:**
- Grants for states to build dashboards (using open-source templates)
- Standardized data formats so third parties can build analysis tools
- Mandatory for all federal elections by 2028

---

### Pillar 4: **Independent Security Audits**

**The Reform:**  
Require pre-election security audits by independent cybersecurity firms (think: "penetration testing" for election systems).

**Why It Works:**  
- **Proactive:** Finds vulnerabilities before bad actors do
- **Credible:** Third-party audits are more trustworthy than self-assessment
- **Accountable:** Auditors can be held liable for failures

**Implementation:**
- Federal registry of certified election security auditors
- Mandatory audits 90 days before federal elections
- Public release of audit summaries (technical details withheld until vulnerabilities are patched)

**Cost:** ~$100M annually (about what the Pentagon spends in 6 hours)

---

### Pillar 5: **Transparent Incident Reporting**

**The Reform:**  
Mandatory public reporting of election security incidents within 48 hours:

- Equipment failures
- Cyber intrusion attempts
- Chain-of-custody breaches
- Voter registration anomalies

**Why It Works:**  
- **Early detection:** Transparency helps identify patterns
- **Accountability:** Officials can't sweep problems under the rug
- **Public trust:** Honesty about problems is more trustworthy than denial

**Implementation:**
- Federal incident reporting system (similar to CDC disease tracking)
- Whistleblower protections for election workers who report issues
- Penalties for jurisdictions that fail to report incidents

## Why This Should Be Bipartisan

**For Conservatives:**  
You get verification, transparency, and constitutional adherence. No more "trust the system" gaslighting.

**For Progressives:**  
You get proof that elections are secure, which delegitimizes bad-faith challenges. Plus, open-source and public oversight align with transparency values.

**For Independents:**  
You get a system designed for accountability, not party advantage.

---

## The Real Resistance Won't Be Partisan—It'll Be Bureaucratic

The biggest obstacle to these reforms isn't Democrats or Republicans. It's **the election-industrial complex:**

- Vendors who profit from proprietary systems
- Officials who fear transparency will expose incompetence
- Bureaucrats who see reform as criticism of their work

This is why I need your help. These reforms won't pass because they're good ideas. They'll pass when **voters demand them loudly enough that politicians have no choice.**
