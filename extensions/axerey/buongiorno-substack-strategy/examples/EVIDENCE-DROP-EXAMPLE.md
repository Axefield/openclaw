## Document #1: The Initial Complaint (February 2022)

📄 **[Court Filing Available Here - Placeholder for actual link]**

### What I Alleged:

In my complaint, I documented three critical violations:

1. **Improper Chain of Custody for Ballot Images**  
   Florida Statute §101.68 requires that ballot images (digital scans of every vote) must be preserved and accessible for public inspection. During my investigation, I found gaps in the documented chain of custody.

2. **Inadequate Security Protocols for Election Equipment**  
   Voting machines and tabulators were not maintained according to manufacturer specifications and federal guidelines. Specific devices showed signs of unauthorized access.

3. **Refusal to Comply with Public Records Requests**  
   Despite Florida's Sunshine Laws, election officials delayed and denied access to records I was legally entitled to inspect.

### Why This Matters:

Election security isn't about proving fraud happened—it's about proving **fraud couldn't happen**. When officials can't demonstrate basic security practices, they're asking you to "trust them" instead of showing you the evidence.

That's not transparency. That's blind faith.

## Document #3: The Expert Witness Report (July 2022)

📄 **[Expert Report Summary - Placeholder for actual link]**

I brought in Dr. [Name], a former NSA cybersecurity analyst with 20 years of experience, to examine the election systems. His findings included:

### Critical Vulnerabilities Identified:

1. **Outdated Software on 23% of Devices**  
   Several tabulators were running software versions with known security patches that hadn't been applied.

2. **Weak Password Policies**  
   Administrative accounts used default or easily guessable passwords. One machine had the password "admin123."

3. **Lack of Network Segmentation**  
   Election equipment was on the same network as general office computers, creating potential attack vectors.

4. **No Intrusion Detection Systems**  
   There was no automated monitoring to detect if someone was attempting to access systems improperly.

### The Technical Translation:

This is the equivalent of leaving your front door unlocked, hiding the spare key under the mat, and not installing a security camera—then insisting nobody could possibly break in.
