## 🎨 Tone and Style Guide

All examples follow these principles:

### Voice Characteristics:
- **Direct, not defensive:** Confronts issues head-on without apology
- **Evidence-based, not speculative:** Claims are backed by documents, data, or court records
- **Transparent, not evasive:** Acknowledges challenges and costs openly
- **Urgent, not alarmist:** Conveys importance without fearmongering
- **Bipartisan, not partisan:** Frames issues as people vs. establishment, not left vs. right

### Structural Elements:
- **Short paragraphs:** 2-4 sentences max for readability
- **Frequent subheadings:** Visual breaks every 200-300 words
- **Bullet points & tables:** Complex info presented visually
- **Bolded key phrases:** Emphasizes takeaways for scanners
- **Strong CTAs:** Every article ends with specific, actionable next steps

### Rhetorical Devices:
- **Preemptive objection handling:** Anticipates skepticism and addresses it directly
- **Comparative framing:** Shows "current system" vs. "better system"
- **Transparency signaling:** Phrases like "Here's exactly..." and "Full disclosure..."
- **Reader inclusion:** Uses "we" and "you" to build community

## ⚠️ Legal and Compliance Notes

### Fundraising Disclaimers
All donation requests MUST include:
- FEC compliance notice ("Federal law requires...")
- Employer/occupation collection notice (for donations >$200)
- Non-tax-deductible statement
- Maximum contribution limits

**Example footer (include in all fundraising content):**

> _Federal election law requires us to collect your employer and occupation for donations over $200. Contributions are not tax-deductible. Maximum individual contribution: $3,300 per election (primary and general elections are separate)._

---

### Document Sharing
When sharing court documents or public records:
- ✅ **DO:** Share documents obtained through legal means (FOIA, court records, public filings)
- ✅ **DO:** Redact personal information (addresses, SSNs, etc.)
- ❌ **DON'T:** Share sealed or confidential documents
- ❌ **DON'T:** Make claims that aren't supported by the documents

**When in doubt, consult campaign legal counsel before publishing.**

---

### Defamation Protection
When discussing opponents or officials:
- Stick to documented facts
- Avoid speculation about motives or character
- Use phrases like "according to court records" or "as documented in..."
- Never claim someone is "corrupt" without legal adjudication

**The standard:** Could you prove this claim in court using evidence you have?

## 💡 Tips for Writing New Content

### Finding Your Evidence
- Court records: PACER (pacer.gov), local court clerk websites
- Public records: FOIA requests, state sunshine law requests
- Data sources: FEC.gov, census data, government reports
- Expert validation: Bring in credentialed experts to review technical claims

### Maintaining Momentum
- Batch-write content (write multiple articles in one session)
- Repurpose long articles into social media threads
- Turn reader questions into future article topics
- Save research notes for future Evidence Drops

### Engaging Your Audience
- Reply to every comment in the first 24 hours
- Feature reader questions in articles ("A subscriber asked...")
- Highlight donor stories (with permission)
- Create polls to gauge interest in topics
